What is DisruptX?

What This Simulator Shows

This project is an ADHD attention simulator. It demonstrates how focus, distractions, reaction time and time perception can change under ADHD-like conditions compared to a quieter, normal condition.

  • Selective attention: click only on target stimuli.
  • Inhibitory control: avoid clicking non-targets.
  • Reaction time: speed of response to targets.
  • Distraction load: notification popups reduce performance.
  • Working memory: holding digit sequences in mind.
  • Dual-Tasking: managing visual and audio stimuli simultaneously.
  • Stroop Interference: inhibition control against conflicting stimuli.
  • Sustained Attention: tracking performance stability over time.
  • Temporal Stability: plotting fatigue and accuracy decline across trial blocks.
  • Auditory Distractions: comparing focus in silence vs. noise environments.
  • Task Switching: measuring cognitive flexibility and 'switch cost'.
  • Time perception: internal estimate of 5 seconds.

This is only a simulator for educational purposes and does not diagnose ADHD.
